Resistor Girl

I directed the organization of a three-day camp to introduce new technology to girls that combine concepts from the disciplines of engineering, computer science, fashion and design. The aim: to empower girls with the ability to make an informed choice about their future by exposing them to new technologies that interest them.

The three core building blocks of developing this event:

  1. Research & Development: Gaining a strong understanding of the LilyPad Arduino, how to engage girls in the development of LilyPad Arduino projects, planning volunteer and participant engagement
  2. Event & Team Organization: Finding participants, volunteers, sponsorship and local support through a great network and the use of social media 
  3. Going Forward: For those interested in starting up their own workshops, a handy online guide to explain the process

Society of Women Engineers

I served as Chair of the 2010 Society of Women Engineers Region C Conference. Over the course of six months,  I managed and supported several volunteer committees throughout the planning and execution of the conference. What came out was a three day conference with over 300 members in attendance.

  • corporate sponsorship, worked with local and national companies to raise a $60,000 budget
  • vendor and hotel arrangements
  • tours and attractions
  • workshop sessions, meals, keynote dinner
  • registration and conference awareness campaigns
  • designed all materials (all program materials, conference swag, logo, t-shirts) 
  • developed the conference website and maintained our social media presence
